Science Tunnel 3.0: Logbook (iOS App)

Concept of a smart guide app for an exhibition about science and the Max-Planck-Gesellschaft. While walking through the exhibition, you may find interesting things that you want to remember. Instead of taking notes, you can put stamps on your phone using physical stamps with custom multi-touch markers. The app is kept simple and acts as a logbook to keep track of your collection and to offer additional information.

The Library of the Future

with Julian Stahnke, Lionel Michel and Michael Härtel

Following the principles of user-centered design, we prototyped a new way to search for books in libraries that takes advantage of multi-touch technology.

Leaving aside the visual design, we focused on creating personas, interviewing users and experts as well as on the creation and testing of a paper prototype. During the research process, we were able to highlight two user scenarios: an explorative, browsing-oriented search, and an academic-oriented search. We choose to focus on the latter.

Our multi-touch concept was based on a faceted navigation and used innovative data visualizations like treemaps or timelines. During the user tests, it showed potential to replace desktop-based solutions.

Cycle (Mail App for Mac OSX)

Chummy is for personal mail. Cycle is for business mail. We all know the problem of too many mails in our inbox. We can flag and label them but this is just like moving them into folders. You may want to reply later but you often forget that you even received that mail because it gets out of focus.

The core feature of Cycle is the future inbox that keeps track of mails that you marked to review later. You can use a review slider to quickly set a review date (such as later, tomorrow, in 3 days, next week) or define a specific date. The mail will move up in your mail stream and you will get it again. No more forgetting ;)

Text Visualization

I thought about different styles of writing. Therfor I analyzed a text, from smallest units, the characters, to words and their meanings. I created a micro structure to allow users to decode the text while enhancing the information with a color code that represents various word types. Maybe teachers could use it to make clear to students, that, a proper language style is required.

Web of Debt (Visualization)

I received about 100 spreadsheets with data about debts of all countries of the world. I was asked if I could visualize the content in an understandable way. Therefor I used processing to loop through the datasets and plotted some graphs. This would have been a lot of pain to do in Excel or the like. You can see absolut values in time as well as relative values to see the connection between the countries.

Conscape (Multi-touch Timeline)

I developed an interactive timeline tool that lets users define a time range with a zoom gesture. Each bar represents a day with its height showing the number of concerts in Berlin on that day. When you zoom into the timeline you can narrow it down to certain months or weeks. The left and right borders are the first and last dates that are visualized on the map. You can then drag the timeline to travel through the history of concerts in Berlin.

Radial Menu

Task was to create a navigation to view and modify simple graphical animations. I wanted to have a set of tools that would engage users to explore the animations with gestures. The radial menu is super minimalistic; designed with no more than circles.
